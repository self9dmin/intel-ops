# Foundation Track Alignment: Dynatrace Getting Started

**Review date:** 2026-07-19  
**Evidence window:** 2026-01-19 through 2026-07-19  
**Purpose:** Make the first Mission Control circuit mirror the current Dynatrace onboarding path while converting each concept into a practical action, observation, and handoff.

## Executive decision

The current official starting point is not “learn every app.” It is a short operational loop:

1. Start in a trial or Playground.
2. Ingest data with OneAgent or OpenTelemetry.
3. Verify that Dynatrace understands the environment in Services and Smartscape.
4. Explore a concrete question, inspect the generated DQL, and make a reusable dashboard or notebook view.
5. Set up notifications.
6. Investigate with Problems, Logs, Distributed Tracing, and Kubernetes as the use case requires.
7. Continue through Community, University, Developer, and Playground resources.

Mission Control should teach this loop as a series of observable decisions. A learner should leave each lesson with an entity, signal, query, view, or next action that another operator can inspect. A multiple-choice answer may confirm understanding, but it cannot be the only proof of platform skill.

Ground Zero now follows the current official sequence:

| Order | Mission | Practical outcome | Evidence status |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Find Your Footing | Locate navigation, support, developer, and community entry points | Existing content; refresh exact labels before publication |
| 2 | Welcome to the Playground | Understand the platform model and where data, intelligence, and action fit | Existing content; navigation-heavy and under review |
| 3 | Ingest the First Signal | Choose OneAgent or OpenTelemetry and define the first observable success | Documentation-backed; Playground replay pending |
| 4 | Verify the Data | Confirm an entity, signal, relationship, time window, and known gap | Documentation-backed; fixture-specific entity validation pending |
| 5 | Build the First View | Use Explore, inspect generated DQL, and save a dashboard or notebook view | Documentation-backed; permission and fixture validation pending |
| 6 | First Briefing | Start the shift with bounded evidence from Problems and Assist when available | Strengthened foundation bridge; retain visible-data fallback |
| 7 | Your First Alert | Connect a problem to an audience, channel, and verification step | Existing content; current Settings labels and outcome need replay |

`Know Your Wheel` remains available but now sits in Operator Readiness. Its breadth is useful after the learner has a first working loop; it is too wide to be the third stop in a new-user sequence.

## Practical lesson contract

Every new foundation mission should contain these four parts:

1. **Action:** The learner opens the named current surface and performs one bounded task.
2. **Observation:** The learner records an entity, signal, relationship, filter, query, view, or response.
3. **Decision:** The learner chooses a next step based on what was observed.
4. **Fallback:** If the tenant lacks the capability or data, the mission tells the learner how to use the Playground or document the gap instead of guessing.

This is also the content rule for Assist, CLI, SDK, API, and MCP lessons. They should accelerate or reproduce a known operational outcome. They should not become a vocabulary tour detached from the platform evidence the learner is supposed to trust.

## Validation and publication gate

The three new missions are deliberately marked `documentation-backed`, not `playground-validated`. Before changing their evidence status, run each mission against the current Playground or a named tenant fixture and capture:

- the exact route and app label used;
- the visible data mode and time range;
- the entity, signal, query, or saved view observed;
- the required permission or entitlement;
- a screenshot or structured evidence record;
- the fallback behavior when the expected capability is absent.

Do not hard-code sample entity names, counts, dashboard labels, Assist responses, or DQL output into rookie checkpoints until that replay has been completed. The mission can teach the workflow while accepting fixture-specific evidence.
